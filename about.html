ABOUT

My name is Leon Dobson, I was born in London and grew up in the South East. I was a very creative child, making short films by myself and with friends as well as developing a strong passion for music and writing.

I went on to study music at the University of Birmingham, graduating in 2021 with a 2.1 and a thorough grounding in music composition, history and the use of software such as Sibelius and Reaper.

After moving to London and working in hospitality for a while, I enrolled for the filmmaking certificate at the London Film Academy, in which I took part in every stage of filmmaking: writing a script, storyboarding, producing, casting actors, shooting and editing. This was a formative experience that solidified my desire to work in film and creative media.

Shortly after this in August 2022 I secured a production runner internship at Shine TV which evolved into a full-time runner position on the studio set of MasterChef for two series (Young MasterChef Series 1 and MasterChef Series 19). Here I learned about television, how to work with talent, crew, and production, and gained experience in tech roles, handling equipment and observing set etiquette.

Around this time I met another sound recordist Solomon Watkins, who mentored me in recording and recommended me for jobs including my first sound job on a documentary in Dublin.

In 2023 I left MasterChef and undertook a course in sound recording theory and practice at the London Film School to fill in the gaps in my knowledge and gain further advice from other sound professionals.

I have now been working full-time as a freelance sound recordist for the past year, travelling around the UK, meeting so many wonderful and creative people, and discovering my ever increasing creative ambitions and passion for sound.

Currently, I am continuing to strengthen my skills and explore new avenues, particularly in audio post-production and audio-visual work as well as web design (this website was entirely made from scratch using html, css and javascript).
